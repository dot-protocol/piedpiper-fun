I let people send things to each other without a company in the middle.

The show ran for six seasons and ended with the protocol working and the team choosing not to ship it. Nobody believed that was the right call. We are the people who didn't put it down.

Every file you send through WhatsApp, iCloud, or Google Drive passes through a company's server. They can see it, store it, and cut you off. Pied Piper routes around all of that. Your device finds the recipient's device. The relay in Frankfurt, if you use one, cannot read what passes through it.

The code is open source. The relay is self-hostable on a Raspberry Pi. There is no series A, no exit plan, no insider allocation. The people building this hold the same tokens you can buy on the open market.

The trailer was the show. The film is what we ship.
